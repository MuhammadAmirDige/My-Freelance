Overview

The Campaign

This was a full-funnel paid media campaign running simultaneously across three platforms — Meta (Facebook), Google Ads, and TikTok Ads Manager — over a 31-day period in May 2024. The objective was to drive direct sales conversions at the lowest possible cost per acquisition, while testing which platform delivered the strongest ROI for the client's audience.

Each platform was assigned a distinct role in the funnel: TikTok for top-of-funnel discovery, Google for high-intent search capture, and Facebook for retargeting and lookalike audience conversion. The combined spend of $8,934 generated 3,602 total conversions at a blended CPA of $2.48.

Strategy & Insights

What the Numbers Mean

Google Ads
Search Intent Wins on CPA
Google delivered the lowest CPA at $2.36 — confirming that capturing high-intent search traffic converts more efficiently than interruption-based ads. Summer Sale Search Campaign alone drove 782 conversions.
Facebook Ads
Lookalike + Retargeting Power
Facebook's 3-campaign structure (Conversion, Website Visitors, Lookalike Purchasers) maintained consistent CPA at $2.45 while reaching 235K unique people — the strongest reach of all three platforms.
TikTok Ads
Discovery at Scale
TikTok delivered 18,645 destination clicks at 1.83% CTR — strong for a discovery platform. The 18–34 male demographic dominated, indicating a clear audience signal for future creative targeting.
Cross-Platform
Budget Allocation Worked
All three platforms stayed within $0.13 of each other on CPA ($2.36–$2.49) — a sign of well-calibrated budget distribution. No single platform was over-funded or starved of budget to perform.
Key Takeaways

What I Learned

1
A $2.48 blended CPA across three platforms in a 31-day period is a strong result — but the real win is the consistency. When all three channels converge on similar CPAs, it means the audience targeting, creative, and landing pages are aligned across the full funnel.
2
Google's lowest CPA confirms a fundamental rule: intent-based traffic always outperforms cold audience interruption when the objective is direct conversion. For future campaigns, shifting 10–15% more budget to Google Search would likely improve overall blended CPA.
3
TikTok's demographic data (18–34, male-skewed) is the most valuable output of this campaign — not the conversions. This audience signal should directly inform the creative strategy and product positioning for the next campaign cycle.
4
Running all three platforms simultaneously creates attribution overlap — a visitor touched by TikTok, retargeted on Facebook, and converted through Google will be counted as a conversion on all three. Understanding this is critical for honest ROI reporting to clients.
